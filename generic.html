Breast Cancer

Breast cancer is the most common malignancy among women worldwide[1] and a deadly disease. Breast tumors diagnosis has traditionally been performed by invasive surgical procedures. Besides, the majority of women feel persistent post-surgical pain which may persist for several years. But there is hope.

Many parameters may be correlated with malignancy and thus incorporated in artificial intelligence solutions for early diagnoses. In oncology, machine learning models play an important role in developing new auxiliary tools for clinicians. With this in mind I searched to use machine learning techniques in this project in order to predict whether the tumor in my dataset is benign or malign.

[1] Bray F, Ferlay J, Soerjomataram I, et al. Global cancer statistics 2018: GLOBOCAN estimates of incidence and mortality worldwide for 36 cancers in 185 countries. _CA Cancer J Clin_. 2018;68:394–424. doi:10.3322/caac.21492
